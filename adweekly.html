Weekly Ad Report

A view of what each ad campaign is producing — how many appointments it booked, how many accredited investor leads it brought in, and what those results cost. Use it to decide where to put more spend and where to pull back.

What's counted

Intros Booked — meetings classified as "intro" (or "Investor Success Profile" / "ISP"), counted by booking date within the selected range.

Discoveries Booked — meetings classified as "discovery" (HubSpot enum types Discovery Call - Main or Discovery Call - Setters), counted by booking date.

Classification matches the setter and closer dashboards exactly, so totals reconcile across all three.

How attribution works

Every meeting and lead gets attributed to one campaign via a first-touch waterfall. The dashboard walks down this list until it finds a value:

1. hs_analytics_source_data_2 (Drill Down 2) — the specific campaign or ad name. Most actionable.
2. If empty, hs_analytics_source_data_1 (Drill Down 1) — the platform (e.g., "Facebook").
3. If still empty, hs_analytics_source (Original Source) — e.g., "Paid Social", "Direct Traffic".
4. If all three are empty, the row appears as (not set).

Each row has a badge showing which level applied: DD2 (campaign-specific), DD1 (platform only), or SRC (source only). DD2 rows are the most actionable because you know which exact campaign produced the result.

Rows without DD2 still show up — rolled up at the platform or source level — so totals stay honest and reconcile with the setter and closer dashboards. Purely numeric DD2 values are treated as malformed (a legacy integration stamped HubSpot user IDs into DD2 on some older contacts) and fall back to DD1.

For new contacts (created in the period), attribution is read from their earliest form-submission event. For returning contacts (created before the period but who submitted a form during it), it's read from the latest in-period form submission — last touch within the window. Contacts with no form submission at all fall back to their hs_analytics_source* contact properties; this catches manually-created contacts and tracking gaps.

The four charts

Intros and Discoveries pies show top campaigns by booking volume.

New AI Leads pie shows top campaigns by accredited-investor lead volume.

The Quality / Cost bar chart toggles between two views using the buttons in the panel header:

Quality ranks campaigns by accredited share (% AI), highest first.

Cost ranks campaigns by Cost Per AI Lead (CPAIL), cheapest first. Only includes campaigns with both spend AND AI leads.

Campaigns with fewer than 10 total leads are too statistically noisy to rank against larger ones, so they roll up into a single Low Volume bar pinned to the top of the chart (same threshold in both modes). Click that bar to see which campaigns are inside it.

The main table

Each row is one campaign with its Intros, Discoveries, lead counts, AI counts, % AI, Spend, and CPAIL.

Sort: Discoveries Booked descending by default. Click any number-column header to flip. (not set) rows always pin to the top regardless of sort. When sorting by % AI, rows with zero leads (no denominator) sink to the bottom regardless of direction, and equal percentages tiebreak by lead volume — so a 100% rate from 50 leads outranks a 100% rate from 1.

Click any campaign row to open a per-campaign drill drawer. For Meta campaigns, the drawer shows an Ad Set Breakdown table at the top — each ad set within the campaign with its intros, discoveries, total leads, AI leads, spend, and CPAIL. Click any ad set row to expand a nested First Conversion breakdown — each first_conversion_event_name with a count of contacts (new + returning) attributed to that ad set, sorted by count. Below that is the full Attribution Breakdown: Total Leads / New Leads / Returning Leads, then Total AI / New AI / Returning AI — six metrics with Count, % of Total Leads, and CPL each. At the bottom of every drill drawer is a First Conversion Breakdown showing each first_conversion_event_name and its contact count for the entire campaign — this section appears for all campaigns, not just Meta campaigns with ad-set data.

Filters and how to slice the data

Source filter (multi-select dropdown) filters by hs_analytics_source (Original Source). Affects everything — KPIs, charts, table, and totals strip.

Row filter buttons (All / Booked Appts / Leads) narrow the table body and totals strip only. KPIs and charts stay the same.

Click any totals-strip cell to narrow the table to rows that contributed to that number. Click "Intros" to see only campaigns with intros; "New AI Leads" to see only campaigns with new accredited leads. Click the active cell again to clear back to All.

Totals strip and lens pills

The top KPIs (Intros / Discoveries) and the totals strip above the table show similar numbers but answer different questions. KPIs are the period's headline numbers — they reflect the source filter but never the row filter (clicking "Booked Appts" doesn't change them). The totals strip is the sum of whatever's currently in the table — it reflects both source filter and row filter, so it always equals the column sums of the visible rows.

Three lens pills above the totals strip — Total / New / Returning — reshape the four lead-related cells (Leads, AI count, % AI, CPAIL) without leaving the page. Intros, Discoveries, and Spend stay constant across views.

Total view shows Total Leads, Total AI, % Total AI, CPAIL on Total AI.

New view shows New Leads, New AI, % New AI, CPAIL on New AI. Counts contacts created in the period.

Returning view shows Returning Leads, Returning AI, % Returning AI, CPAIL on Returning AI. Counts contacts whose createdate is before the period but who submitted a form during it. Attribution comes from their last in-period form submission.

Cells stay clickable for table drilling in every view, but the drill target follows the lens — clicking the AI cell in New view filters to rows with New AI > 0; in Returning view, to rows with Returning AI > 0. Switching lens resets the row filter to "All" so the table doesn't get stuck in a stale state.

Spend and CPAIL

The Spend column is fetched directly from each ad platform's API per campaign, summed across the active date range. Spend is exact — the platform's reported number, no derivation. Meta, LinkedIn, and Google Ads are integrated.

Rows that aren't paid-platform campaigns — "(not set)", platform-fallback rows (DD1 / SRC) — show "—" in Spend (and consequently "—" in CPAIL).

CPAIL (Cost Per AI Lead) = Spend ÷ Total AI Leads. Shown as "—" when the row has no spend or zero AI leads. The aggregate CPAIL in the totals strip equals sum(Spend across visible rows with spend) ÷ sum(Total AI across those same rows), keeping numerator and denominator at the same scope so the aggregate stays honest.

Spend Without HubSpot Attribution

Lists Meta, LinkedIn, and Google Ads campaigns that spent money in the period but had no HubSpot contact attributed to them. Each row shows the campaign's objective so you can tell at a glance whether the missing attribution is worth investigating or expected.

Sort puts lead-driving campaigns first (Lead Generation, Website Conversions) — these are the ones that should have produced contacts. Engagement, Brand Awareness, and Video Views appear lower because they're not designed to drive direct conversions; missing attribution is normal for them.

This panel ignores the source filter (orphans have no HubSpot record, so source slicing doesn't apply) but respects the platform filter — deselecting Meta, LinkedIn, or Google hides those rows.

Common causes for genuine lead-driving orphans: brand-new campaigns still ramping, audience-split variants whose events haven't flowed to HubSpot yet, or campaigns whose impressions didn't convert.

AI Leads by Source Bucket cards

Three cards between the KPIs and the chart row show Paid, Organic, and Direct lead creation for the active date range, each broken into Yes (accredited investor), No (not accredited), and Unknown (accreditation status blank). Each number shows a green/red comparison delta vs the previous period (same-length window immediately before the selected range).

Source classification uses HubSpot's hs_analytics_source: any value containing "Paid" maps to the Paid bucket, "Direct Traffic" maps to Direct, and everything else (Organic Search, Organic Social, Referral, Email, etc.) maps to Organic.

Click any card to open a three-level drill-down drawer:

Level 1 — Source: Rows grouped by hs_analytics_source value (e.g., "Paid Social", "Organic Search", "Email"), each showing Yes/No/Unknown/Total counts. Click a source row to expand.

Level 2 — Campaign (DD2): Within each source, rows grouped by hs_analytics_source_data_2 (campaign name). Falls back to DD1 (platform) if DD2 is empty. Click a campaign row to expand.

Level 3 — Contacts: Individual contacts with name (linked to HubSpot), created date, and accreditation status.

A Current Period / Previous Period toggle at the top of the drawer lets you switch between the two comparison windows. All data comes from HubSpot contacts already in memory — no extra API calls.

What this dashboard doesn't show yet

Google Ads per-row Spend. Pending API integration. Google campaign rows currently show "—" in Spend and CPAIL until that ships.

Meeting attribution from events. Intros and Discoveries still resolve attribution from contact properties, not events — meeting-having contacts can have createdates from years ago, and fetching full event histories for them is a much bigger scope.

Funded investments. Not included here. Investment attribution arguably belongs on the Closed Won milestone-stamped property (traffic_source_2_before_closed_won) rather than first-touch, which is a separate design decision.

Caching. Meetings and contacts are read from the nightly R2 cache (same source as the other dashboards). Engagement events still fetch live from HubSpot every load, as do Meta, LinkedIn, and Google Ads API calls. Loads run 30–90 seconds depending on date range.

Closing drawers

The Guide, the Low Volume drawer, and the per-campaign drill drawer all close on Esc, on the × button, or by clicking outside.
