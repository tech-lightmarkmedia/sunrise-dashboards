Web Traffic Dashboard — Guide

What this dashboard shows

Website traffic from Google Analytics 4, broken down by channel, page, and UTM campaign. This is all site visitors — including anonymous traffic that never becomes a HubSpot contact. The other SCI dashboards only see contacts who filled out a form; this one covers everything upstream of that.

Data source

Google Analytics 4 property (ID 324853644) via the GA4 Data API. Data is fetched live on each load — not cached. Refresh cadence depends on GA4's own processing pipeline (typically 24–48 hours for finalized data; recent data may shift slightly).

KPI tiles

Sessions: Total sessions in the period. A session is a group of user interactions within a time frame (default 30 minutes of inactivity ends a session).

Users: Total active users (unique visitors identified by cookie/device).

New Users: Users visiting for the first time in the period.

Avg Duration: Average engagement time per session (time the page was in the foreground).

Engagement Rate: Percentage of sessions that were "engaged" — lasted longer than 10 seconds, had a conversion event, or had 2+ page views.

Pageviews: Total page views (one page load = one pageview, even revisits).

Traffic by Channel

Rows are GA4's default channel groupings: Organic Search, Paid Social, Direct, Referral, Organic Social, Email, etc. Click any row to drill into the top pages for that channel.

Top Pages

Most-viewed pages across all channels. The "Page" column shows the URL path (e.g., /fund-5). Click a row to drill into which channels drove traffic to that page.

UTM Campaigns

Traffic grouped by the utm_campaign parameter. Only appears for traffic that arrived with UTM tags. "(not set)" means no campaign tag was present. Click a row to drill into pages within that campaign.

Drill drawer

Click any row in any table to open a drill-down panel on the right showing the next level of detail for that row.

Date ranges

All ranges are relative to today. "Last 30 Days" means the 30 days ending yesterday (GA4 convention — today's data is partial). Custom dates use the date pickers; click Refresh to apply.
