Web Traffic Dashboard — Guide

What this dashboard shows

Website traffic from Google Analytics 4, broken down by source, channel, page, and UTM campaign. This is all site visitors — including anonymous traffic that never becomes a HubSpot contact. The other SCI dashboards only see contacts who filled out a form; this one covers everything upstream of that.

Data source

Google Analytics 4 property (ID 324853644) via the GA4 Data API. Data is fetched live on each load — not cached. Refresh cadence depends on GA4's own processing pipeline (typically 24–48 hours for finalized data; recent data may shift slightly).

Layout

The top hero block shows KPI tiles (3×2 grid) on the left and the Sessions by Source doughnut on the right. Below that: the channel table, top pages, and UTM campaigns.

Traffic filter (All / Paid / Organic / Direct)

The toggle bar filters the entire dashboard to a traffic bucket. All shows everything. Paid includes Paid Social, Paid Search, Paid Shopping, Paid Video, and Paid Other. Direct is direct traffic only. Organic is everything else — Organic Social, Organic Search, Organic Video, Email, Referral, and any other non-paid, non-direct channels.

When a bucket is selected, KPIs re-aggregate to that bucket, the channel table filters, the Sessions by Source doughnut re-renders with only sources from that bucket, and Top Pages / UTM Campaigns re-fetch from GA4 scoped to the selected channels. Results are cached per bucket — switching back is instant.

Note: Avg Duration shows "—" for non-All buckets because GA4 doesn't provide per-channel session duration in the channel breakdown. All other KPIs are accurately re-aggregated. Engagement Rate is weighted by sessions.

Sessions by Source (doughnut)

The doughnut chart shows sessions grouped by consolidated source. Raw GA4 sources (e.g., facebook, fb, l.facebook.com, m.facebook.com) are rolled up into logical groups so you can see the real picture at a glance. The doughnut responds to the traffic filter — selecting Paid/Organic/Direct re-fetches sources scoped to that bucket. The groups are:

Facebook — facebook, fb, l.facebook.com, m.facebook.com, facebook.com, ig, Instagram, instagram.com, lm.facebook.com, business.facebook.com, and related variants. Includes Instagram (same parent company and ad platform).

Google — google, google.com, adwords, sites.google.com, mail.google.com, docs.google.com, and related Google properties.

(direct) — (direct), (not set), (data not available). Traffic where the referrer is unknown.

SCI (self) — sunrisecapitalinvestors.com, the InvestNext subdomain, and HubSpot preview/hosted pages. Internal self-referral traffic.

HubSpot — hs_email, hs_automation, meetings.hubspot.com, app.hubspot.com, and HubSpot editor previews. Traffic from HubSpot email campaigns and workflows.

LinkedIn — LinkedIn, linkedin, linkedin.com, lnkd.in, and case variants.

YouTube — YouTube, youtube.com.

Other Search — bing, yahoo, duckduckgo, ecosia.org, aol. Non-Google search engines.

AI — chatgpt.com, perplexity.ai, claude.ai, gemini.google.com. Traffic from AI assistant tools.

Other — everything that doesn't match a named group.

Click any slice or legend row to open a drill drawer with three toggles: Source Detail (the raw source breakdown within that group — e.g., l.facebook.com: 7,452 / facebook: 4,607 / m.facebook.com: 1,294), Pages (top pages visited by that source group), and UTM Campaigns (campaigns tagged to that source group).

KPI tiles

Sessions: Total sessions in the period. A session is a group of user interactions within a time frame (default 30 minutes of inactivity ends a session).

Users: Total active users (unique visitors identified by cookie/device).

New Users: Users visiting for the first time in the period.

Avg Duration: Average engagement time per session (time the page was in the foreground). Only available in the All view.

Engagement Rate: Percentage of sessions that were "engaged" — lasted longer than 10 seconds, had a conversion event, or had 2+ page views.

Pageviews: Total page views (one page load = one pageview, even revisits).

Period comparison

The vs dropdown enables comparison against the previous period (same-length window immediately before the selected range). When active, KPI tiles show green/red deltas (percentage change for counts, percentage-point change for rates), and the channel table adds a Δ Sessions column showing per-channel change. Set to "None" to hide comparisons.

Traffic by Channel

Rows are GA4's default channel groupings. Filtered by the active traffic bucket. Each row shows a short description of what the channel means. Click any column header to sort. Click any row to open a drill drawer with Pages / UTM Campaigns / Sources toggles.

Channel definitions

These are GA4's standard channel groupings. GA4 classifies each session into exactly one channel based on how the visitor arrived.

Organic Search — unpaid search engine results (Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, etc.).

Organic Social — unpaid social media links (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, X/Twitter, YouTube, TikTok, etc.).

Organic Video — unpaid video platform links (YouTube, Vimeo, etc.) when GA4 distinguishes it from Organic Social.

Paid Social — paid ads on social platforms (Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads, etc.).

Paid Search — paid search ads (Google Ads search, Bing Ads, etc.).

Direct — direct URL entry, bookmarks, or links with no referrer information.

Referral — links from other websites that aren't search engines or social platforms.

Email — links from email campaigns (UTM medium = "email" or a recognized email platform referrer).

Cross-network — multi-network paid campaigns (Google Ads Performance Max, Smart Shopping, etc.). This is paid traffic.

Unassigned — GA4 couldn't classify the session into any standard channel.

AI Assistant — traffic from AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overview, etc.).

Top Pages

Most-viewed pages, scoped to the active traffic bucket. The "Page" column shows the URL path (e.g., /fund-5). Click a row to drill into which channels drove traffic to that page.

UTM Campaigns

Traffic grouped by the utm_campaign parameter, scoped to the active traffic bucket. "(not set)" means no campaign tag was present. Click a row to drill into pages within that campaign.

Drill drawers

There are two types of drill drawers:

Channel drill (from the channel table): Three toggles — Pages (top pages for that channel), UTM Campaigns (campaigns for that channel), and Sources (source/medium pairs showing exactly where within a channel the traffic originates, e.g., youtube.com / referral, google / cpc).

Source group drill (from the doughnut): Three toggles — Source Detail (raw GA4 sources within the group with sessions/users/new users), Pages (top pages visited by that source group), and UTM Campaigns (campaigns for that source group).

All views are fetched in parallel when the drawer opens and cached for instant toggling. The drawer closes via the × button or Esc key.

Date ranges

Default: Last Month. Preset ranges include Today, This Week, Last Week, This Month, Last Month, This Quarter, Last Quarter, This Year, Last Year, and rolling windows (Last 7/14/30/90 Days, Last 12 Months). Custom dates use the date pickers; click Refresh to apply. Changing any preset automatically refreshes. "Last N Days" ranges end yesterday (GA4 convention — today's data is partial).
