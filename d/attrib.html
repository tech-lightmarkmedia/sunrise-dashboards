UTM Attribution — Guide

What This Dashboard Shows

Every contact's first (or last) landing URL is stored by HubSpot with all query parameters intact — including utm_source, utm_medium, and utm_campaign. This dashboard parses those URLs directly instead of relying on HubSpot's DD1/DD2 source categorization, which loses information through inconsistent mapping across source categories.

Each row is a unique source + medium + campaign combination. The columns show how many contacts (leads and AI leads) arrived through that combination, and what those contacts went on to produce: discovery calls, commitments, and funded investments.

First Touch vs. Last Touch

First Touch parses hs_analytics_first_url — the URL from the contact's very first tracked visit. For contacts imported before HubSpot tracking was active (typically ~March 2023), this represents their first HubSpot-tracked interaction, not necessarily their original acquisition source.

Last Touch parses hs_analytics_last_url — the URL from the contact's most recent session. This shows what brought them back most recently, which may differ from how they originally arrived.

The † Fallback Indicator

Rows marked with † could not be attributed from the URL (the field was empty or contained no utm_campaign). These fall back to HubSpot's Original Source and DD1/DD2 fields. The "URL Attribution Rate" KPI shows what percentage of contacts have parseable UTM data in their URL — higher is better.

Funnel Columns

Discoveries: Discovery call meetings (HubSpot meetings with a discovery outcome) associated with contacts in that campaign row. From the R2 meetings cache.

Commitments: Deals that entered the InvestNext commit stage, associated with contacts in that campaign row. Fetched live.

Investments: Funded investment records associated with contacts in that campaign row. From the R2 investments cache.

These columns show the full-funnel outcome of each campaign's leads — not just volume, but what that volume produced.

Data Source

Contacts are fetched live from HubSpot (chunked by month to handle volume). Meetings and investments are read from the nightly R2 cache. Commitments are fetched live. All data is joined through contact associations.
