UTM Attribution — Guide

What This Dashboard Shows

Every contact's first (or last) landing URL is stored by HubSpot with all query parameters intact — including utm_source, utm_medium, and utm_campaign. This dashboard parses those URLs directly instead of relying on HubSpot's DD1/DD2 source categorization, which loses information through inconsistent mapping across source categories.

Each row is a unique source + medium + campaign combination. The columns show how many contacts arrived through that combination, and what those contacts went on to produce.

First Touch vs. Last Touch

First Touch parses hs_analytics_first_url — the URL from the contact's very first tracked visit. For contacts imported before HubSpot tracking (~March 2023), this represents their first HubSpot-tracked interaction, not necessarily their original acquisition source.

Last Touch parses hs_analytics_last_url — the URL from the contact's most recent session.

The † Fallback Indicator

Rows marked with † could not be attributed from the URL. These fall back to HubSpot's Original Source and DD1/DD2 fields. The "URL Attribution Rate" KPI shows what percentage of contacts have parseable UTM data.

Filters

Use the Source and Medium dropdowns to filter the table to specific platforms or channels. Multi-select: check/uncheck individual values. Use "All" or "None" for bulk selection. Active filters show an orange badge with the count of selected values. KPIs update to reflect the filtered view.

Funnel Columns

Discoveries: Discovery call meetings associated with contacts in that campaign row. Click the number to see the records.

Commitments: Deals that entered the InvestNext commit stage, associated with contacts in that row.

Investments: Funded investment records associated with contacts in that row.

Data Source

Contacts fetched live from HubSpot (chunked by month). Meetings and investments from the nightly R2 cache. Commitments fetched live. All joined through contact associations.
